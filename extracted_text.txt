### The Scan That Changes Everything — and the Write-Back That Closes the Loop ###
Every existing veterinary inventory system has a critical blind spot at receiving: products arrive, get put on the shelf, and the connection between what was ordered and what was actually received — including the specific lot number and expiration date — is captured manually, if at all. MedSync closes that gap with a single barcode scan at receiving. The dispense side of the equation belongs to VetSpire — that is the point of sale, where the patient record and the clinical invoice live. MedSync does not duplicate that workflow. Instead, it reads the updated on-hand count from VetSpire automatically after each dispense and attributes the depletion to the correct lot using FIFO. The result: MedSync always knows exactly how much product is on the shelf and which lot it came from — without adding any workflow to the clinical team at the point of fill.
The scan at receiving is the foundation of everything that follows. Every lot record, every carrying time calculation, every expiration alert, every recall trace — all of it begins the moment a product is scanned into the delivery. If the receiving data is wrong, everything downstream is wrong. The scan makes it right, automatically, every time.

### A Weekly Count Process That Works in a Clinical Environment ###
A full physical inventory count shuts down the floor, burns two hours, and produces numbers that are already drifting by the time the count is done. Cycle counting is the professional alternative — count a small portion of the formulary each week, on a rotating schedule, so every product is verified regularly without anyone leaving patient care for an extended period.
MedSync automates the cycle count schedule entirely. The Inventory Lead never decides what to count — the platform generates the week's count list based on product value, usage velocity, expiration risk, and historical discrepancy. The IL counts, confirms, and the system handles the rest.

### Three count categories — assigned by MedSync: ###
MedSync automatically assigns every product in the formulary to one of three categories based on clinical risk and product type. Category A covers controlled substances, scheduled drugs, vaccines, and emergency medications — the formulary items where a discrepancy has the highest clinical and regulatory consequence. Category B covers the core formulary: prescription medications, specialty drugs, and diagnostics not captured by A or C. Category C covers supplies, preventatives, and OTC topicals where the cost of an error is lowest. Category determines count frequency — and because category assignment is keyword-driven rather than manual, every new product added to the formulary is automatically classified the moment it enters the system.

### The count schedule — by category: ###
Category A counts every week — no exceptions. Controlled substances and emergency drugs are too high-risk to leave unchecked for any longer than seven days. Category B counts biweekly, during weeks 1 and 3 of each calendar month. Category C counts biweekly, during weeks 2 and 4. Category D — white goods, supplies, and equipment — counts monthly, during week 4 only. The total count load at any given location is manageable in a single session — typically under 30 minutes — because the four-category structure keeps the weekly list focused on the products that matter most right now.
