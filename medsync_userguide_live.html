Clinic Staff Guide — Daily Inventory Operations
✓ Certified

This guide covers everything clinic staff need to know to use MedSync day-to-day. MedSync keeps your inventory accurate, catches discrepancies early, and syncs directly with Vetspire so you're never double-entering data.

🔗 Vetspire Integration: Dispensing a medication in Vetspire automatically decrements the MedSync count. No manual adjustment needed for dispensed items.

Your Dashboard (Staff Landing Screen)
1
Logging in
Go to medsync.vet → click Staff Login. Enter the email and password your admin provided. You land directly on your assigned role's screen — staff lands on Cycle Count by default.
2
Navigating with the sidebar
Tap the ☰ menu icon in the top-left to open the sidebar. Every screen you have access to is listed there. Your name, location, and role appear at the top of the menu.
3
Location context
All data you see — inventory counts, cycle counts, orders — is scoped to your assigned location only. If you're assigned to multiple locations, a location switcher appears at the top of each screen.
🔄 Cycle Count Screen

Cycle counts are routine physical inventory audits. Your admin assigns which category you're counting. Counts keep the system honest and catch shrinkage early.

1
Open Cycle Count from the sidebar
The screen shows your active count assignment (e.g., "Controlled Substances — Week 24"). You'll see every item in that category with its system quantity and a blank field for your physical count.
2
Count each item physically, then enter the number
Walk the shelves with a clipboard or your phone. Count what's physically there — unopened, sealed stock only. Enter the number in the count field next to each item. Do not estimate.
⏱ Most cycle counts take 10–20 minutes per category
3
Review variances highlighted in red
Items where your count differs from the system count turn red automatically. Tap the item to expand and add a note explaining the discrepancy (e.g., "1 vial dropped and discarded", "packaging damaged"). Always note the reason — never leave red items blank.
4
Submit the count
Tap Submit Count at the bottom. Your admin receives an automatic notification for any flagged variances. You'll see a green confirmation screen. The inventory is updated immediately.
✓ Do not re-count after submitting — contact your admin if you made an error
⚠️ Do not adjust counts for items dispensed in Vetspire. The Vetspire sync handles those automatically. Only enter what you physically counted.
📦 Receiving Screen

Use the Receiving screen when a shipment arrives. This adds the new stock to inventory and records lot numbers and expiration dates for traceability.

1
Open Receiving from the sidebar
You'll see a list of open purchase orders (POs) for your location. Find the PO that matches the packing slip in your delivery. If no PO exists, tap + New Receipt to create one manually.
2
Enter quantities received
For each line item, enter: the quantity received, lot number (from the package label), and expiration date. If a product has multiple lots in one shipment, add each lot separately using the + Add Lot button.
3
Flag discrepancies before confirming
If an item was shorted (ordered 10, received 7) or arrived damaged, tap Flag Issue on that line. Select the issue type and add a note. Your admin and the procurement team are notified immediately. Do not complete receiving on damaged goods without flagging them first.
4
Complete receiving
Once all items are entered, tap Complete Receiving. The inventory count updates instantly and the PO is closed. A receipt record is saved automatically.
✓ Inventory updates in real time — no nightly sync needed
🛒 Weekly Order Screen

The Weekly Order screen builds your location's reorder list based on current inventory levels and par quantities. This is where you request new stock.

1
Review suggested order quantities
MedSync pre-fills order quantities based on your par levels and current stock. Items below par are highlighted. Review the list and adjust quantities if needed based on upcoming procedures or promotions.
2
Add or remove items
Tap + Add Item to add anything not already on the suggested list. To remove an item, set its quantity to zero. Items with a quantity of zero are excluded from the order.
3
Review the Vetcove Import tab (if applicable)
If your location uses Vetcove, the ↑ Vetcove Import option in the sidebar lets you upload a Vetcove CSV directly. MedSync maps the items and quantities automatically. Use this to avoid retyping large orders.
4
Submit the order
Tap Place Order. A purchase order is created and routed to your admin for approval. You'll receive a confirmation when the order is approved and sent to the supplier.
⚠️ Goods Lost Screen

Use Goods Lost to report inventory that was damaged, expired, dropped, or otherwise lost outside of normal dispensing. This maintains inventory accuracy without triggering a purchase order.

1
Open Goods Lost from the sidebar
Select the item from the inventory list or search by name. Enter the quantity lost and the date the loss occurred (today, or backdate if discovered late).
2
Select the reason
Choose from: Expired, Damaged/Dropped, Contaminated, Recalled, or Other. For "Other," a note is required. Be specific — this data is used in quarterly loss reports.
3
Submit the report
Tap Submit. The inventory count decreases immediately and an admin notification is sent. The record is permanent and visible in the Activity Log.
✓ Goods Lost records are auditable — never delete or edit after submission
⚠️ Controlled substances: Any loss of a controlled substance must also be reported to your supervising doctor and documented per DEA requirements. MedSync records the event but does not replace regulatory reporting.
Tips & Best Practices
✅ Accuracy goal: 99%. Even small variances compound. When in doubt, count again before submitting — you can't edit after submit.
⚠️ Never skip lot numbers and expiration dates when receiving. Lot lifecycle tracking depends on this data for recall alerts and expiration notifications.
💡 Dispensed in Vetspire already? Don't adjust it in MedSync. The Vetspire sync handles it automatically within a few minutes — adjusting manually creates a double-deduction.
🗓️ Complete your onboarding quiz within 30 days. A countdown appears at the top of this page. Passing earns your certification badge and is visible to your admin in the Training Log.

🏠 Portfolio Dashboard

Your landing screen after login. The Portfolio gives you an organization-wide snapshot — inventory health, recent activity, and alerts across all locations at a glance.

1
Overview cards
The top row shows: total active locations, pending cycle counts, items below par, and expiring lots within 60 days. Red indicators mean action is needed. Tap any card to drill into that view.
2
Location filter
Use the Region / Location dropdown at the top to scope the portfolio to a specific region or clinic. "All Locations" is the default. Saved filter preferences persist between sessions.
3
Recognition & compliance scores
The bottom of the portfolio shows staff recognition data and cycle count compliance rates by location. Locations with low compliance are highlighted so you can follow up with managers directly.
📊 Analytics & Quarterly Reports

Analytics shows spend trends, inventory value, and supplier breakdowns across your organization. Quarterly Report generates a printable one-page summary for ownership or board review.

1
Select Analytics from the sidebar
Choose a date range (month-to-date, last quarter, custom) and filter by location or aggregate across all. The spend chart shows drug vs. supply costs month-over-month.
2
Supplier Breakdown tab
Shows what percentage of spend goes to each distributor (MWI, Covetrus, Vetcove, etc.). Use this to identify consolidation opportunities and negotiate volume pricing.
3
Top Items by Cost
Ranks your highest-cost items by total spend in the period. Flag items where cost has increased significantly and use the Procurement screen to source alternatives.
4
Quarterly Report
Open Quarterly Report from the sidebar. Select the quarter and locations. The report includes: total inventory value, spend by category, goods lost totals, cycle count compliance, and month-over-month comparison. Tap Export PDF to download.
📥 PDF exports are formatted for printing — A4/letter size with MedSync branding
🔬 Lot Lifecycle Screen

Lot Lifecycle tracks every product lot from receiving through depletion, expiration, or recall. Critical for regulatory compliance and managing drug recalls.

1
Expiring lots view
The default view shows lots expiring within 60 days, sorted by soonest expiry. Red = expiring within 30 days, yellow = 31–60 days. Tap a lot to see which locations are holding it and how much remains.
2
Search a specific lot number
Use the search bar to find a specific lot number or NDC. This is the primary tool for handling manufacturer recalls — search the recalled lot and see instantly which locations received it and how much was used vs. remains.
3
Initiating a recall flag
If a recalled lot is found, tap Flag for Recall. This sends an immediate notification to the location manager and marks the remaining stock as quarantined. A Goods Lost report is auto-generated for the quarantined quantity.
🛍️ Procurement Screen

Procurement manages your supplier catalog, purchase order history, and pricing. Use it to approve orders, set par levels, and review supplier performance.

1
Pending approvals
Orders submitted by managers and staff appear here for admin approval. Review the items and quantities, then tap Approve to send to the supplier or Reject with a note to return it for revision.
2
Supplier management
The Suppliers tab lists your active distributors with contact info, account numbers, and order minimums. Add a new supplier with + Add Supplier. Inactive suppliers can be archived (not deleted) to preserve PO history.
3
Setting and updating par levels
Open the Par Levels tab. Search by item and location to see current pars. Tap any row to edit. Changes take effect immediately — the next weekly order will use the new par. Setting pars to zero removes the item from suggested orders.
4
PO history
The Order History tab shows all purchase orders by status: Draft, Pending, Approved, Received, or Flagged. Use the date and location filters to narrow results. Export to CSV for accounting reconciliation.
🕐 Activity Log

The Activity Log is a complete, immutable audit trail of every action taken in MedSync — counts submitted, stock received, users added, goods lost, etc.

1
Filter by type, user, or location
Use the filters at the top to narrow to a specific action type (Cycle Count, Receiving, Goods Lost, User Change, etc.), user, location, or date range. Combine filters for precise lookups.
2
Investigate a discrepancy
If a cycle count shows unexpected variances, filter the Activity Log by that location and the relevant date range. You can see every count, receiving event, and goods lost report that touched the item's quantity.
3
Export for auditing
Tap Export CSV to download the filtered log. Useful for DEA audits (controlled substances), insurance claims, or internal investigations. Records cannot be deleted or modified.
🛒📦🔄 Operations Screens (Order, Receiving, Cycle Count, Goods Lost)

Admins have full access to all operational screens across every location. These work identically to the Staff guide above, with the addition of cross-location visibility.

★
Admin difference: cross-location view
As an admin, each operations screen shows a Location selector at the top. You can view and submit on behalf of any location — useful when a manager is unavailable or when performing a surprise audit count.
★
Vetcove Import (↑ in sidebar)
The Vetcove Import sub-item under Weekly Order accepts a Vetcove-exported CSV. MedSync maps line items to inventory records automatically. Use this for locations that order through Vetcove to save manual entry time.
💡 See the Staff Guide above for step-by-step instructions for Cycle Count, Receiving, Weekly Order, and Goods Lost — the workflow is identical for admins, just with extra location controls.
🧾 Invoice Reconciliation

Match supplier invoices to purchase orders. Identifies billing discrepancies before you pay — overcharges, missed credits, and price changes.

1
Upload or link an invoice
Tap + New Reconciliation. Select the supplier and upload the invoice PDF or enter totals manually. MedSync matches it to the corresponding PO automatically if one exists.
2
Review line-by-line matches
Green rows = invoice matches the PO. Yellow = minor difference (usually rounding). Red = significant mismatch — you ordered 10 units at $12 each but were billed for 10 at $14. Tap any red row to annotate and flag for follow-up.
3
Approve or dispute
Once reviewed, mark the reconciliation as Approved (send to accounts payable) or Disputed (flag the specific lines for your distributor rep). Disputed invoices stay in the queue until resolved.
👥 Managing Users
1
Open Manage Users from the sidebar
You'll see all users in your organization with their role, assigned location(s), and last active date. Use the search bar to find a specific person quickly.
2
Invite a new user
Tap + New User. Enter their full name, work email, and role. Assign one or more locations. Tap Send Invite — they receive a welcome email with a temporary password and a link to set their own.
3
Role guide
Staff — Cycle Count, Receiving, Weekly Order, Goods Lost, Activity Log (view).
Manager — All staff screens + Invoice Reconciliation + limited Manage Users.
Inventory Lead — Manager screens + Analytics (location) + Lot Lifecycle.
Receiver — Receiving and Goods Lost only.
Admin — Full access, including billing and New Location.
4
Edit, reset, or deactivate
Click a user's name to open their profile. You can change their role, reassign locations, send a password reset email, or deactivate their account. Deactivation is immediate — their session is revoked instantly. Historical data is preserved.
🏗️ New Location Setup
1
Check your plan's location limit
Open New Location Setup from the sidebar. Your current plan and remaining location slots are shown at the top. If you're at 0 remaining, you'll see an upgrade prompt before continuing.
2
Fill in location details
Enter: location name, physical address, primary contact name and email, and Vetspire location ID (found in Vetspire under Settings → Locations). Assign to an existing region or create a new one.
3
Submit the request
Tap Submit Setup Request. MedSync provisions the new location within one business day, sets up its inventory schema, and sends a confirmation email to the primary contact. The location appears in your portfolio immediately as "Pending."
4
Upgrading your plan to add more locations
When you're near your limit, an Upgrade Package button appears. This launches a secure Stripe checkout. Select your new tier — the updated location limit is active the moment payment completes. No manual approval needed.
💳 Plan upgrades are instant — new slots available immediately after checkout
🗺️ Manage Regions

Regions let you group locations for easier filtering in Analytics and the Portfolio. For example: "Northeast Clinics", "Texas Locations", "Emergency Hospitals."

1
Create a region
Open Manage Regions → tap + New Region. Give it a name. Once created, you can assign any location to it from either the New Location Setup screen or by editing an existing location.
2
Reassign locations
Click an existing region to expand it. Drag locations between regions or use the location's dropdown to reassign. Changes take effect immediately and update the region filter in Analytics and Portfolio.
⚙️ Settings

Organization-wide preferences: notification thresholds, Vetspire API token management, cycle count frequency, and display preferences.

1
Notification settings
Set the thresholds that trigger admin email alerts: items below par (default: below 20% of par), expiring lots (default: within 45 days), cycle count overdue (default: 3 days past schedule), and goods lost above a dollar threshold.
2
Vetspire integration
The Integrations tab shows your Vetspire API connection status. If the sync is showing errors, you can re-enter the API token here. Changes take effect on the next sync cycle (runs every 15 minutes).
3
Cycle count schedule
Set how frequently each item category must be counted: daily (controlled substances), weekly (vaccines), monthly (general supplies), or quarterly (capital equipment). Staff are notified when a count is due for their location.
📋 Training Log

The Training Log (in the sidebar under User Guide) shows which staff members have completed their onboarding quiz and earned certification. Admin view shows all staff across all locations.

1
Open Training Log from the sidebar
The admin view shows a table of all quiz completions: staff name, location, quiz section, score, and completion date. Filter by location, section, or date range using the controls at the top.
2
Following up on incomplete training
Staff not appearing in the Training Log haven't completed their quiz yet. Use the Manage Users screen to resend the welcome email with a reminder to complete the User Guide quiz within 30 days of account creation.

Platform Admin Guide — Provisioning & Support
✓ Certified

This section is for MedSync internal staff with medsync_superadmin access. It covers provisioning new customer organizations, managing billing records, and handling support escalations through the Platform Admin portal.

🔒 Restricted access. The Platform Admin portal is at medsync_platform_admin.html and requires a medsync_superadmin role. Never share login credentials. Each team member has their own account.
Provisioning a New Organization
1
Log in to Platform Admin
Navigate to medsync_platform_admin.html and log in with your MedSync superadmin credentials. You'll land on the Organizations dashboard.
2
Click "Provision New Organization"
Fill in the organization name, admin name, admin email, tier (plan), and Vetspire API token if provided. Review all fields before proceeding.
3
Step through the provisioning wizard
Step 1: Create Supabase auth account.
Step 2: Create public profile in users table.
Step 3: Create organizations record with tier and limit.
Step 4: Send welcome email with credentials (automatic).
✉️ Welcome email is sent automatically at step 4
4
Confirm and record
After provisioning, the organization appears in the org list. Verify the tier, location limit, and that the admin's role shows as admin. Log the provisioning event in the activity log.
Support Escalation Workflows
1
User locked out / can't log in
In Platform Admin → find the organization → Users tab → find the user → tap Reset Password. This sends a Supabase-triggered password reset email to the user's inbox immediately. If the email domain is wrong, update the email address first, then reset.
2
Billing issue — tier mismatch
In Platform Admin → select the organization → Billing tab. Review the change log. If the tier doesn't match what Stripe shows, use the Update Tier control to manually correct it and note the reason. Then verify in Stripe that the subscription is active and matches.
3
Location limit not updated after payment
Check the Stripe webhook log in the Supabase Edge Function dashboard. If the webhook fired but the org limit didn't update, manually update the location_limit field in the organizations table via Platform Admin → Organization → Edit. Note the reason and Stripe session ID.
4
Vetspire sync failure reported
Check the Vetspire API token for the org in Platform Admin → Integrations. Test the token by running a manual sync. If the token has expired, the admin must generate a new one in Vetspire and update it in MedSync Settings. Sync runs every 15 minutes — confirm it recovers automatically after the token is updated.
5
Suspending an organization
Use the Suspend Org toggle in Platform Admin only for non-payment or Terms of Service violations. Document the reason in the notes field. Suspension immediately revokes all user sessions for that org. Email the admin at the org before suspending unless it is an emergency.
💳 Billing & Stripe Reference
🔐 The Stripe secret key lives only in the Supabase Edge Function secret. Never put it in any HTML file, browser console, or chat. The anon key in HTML is fine — the secret key is not.
1
Checkout flow overview
When a customer upgrades, the create-checkout Edge Function (Supabase → Deno) creates a Stripe Checkout Session with: the selected subscription price ID, a $500 one-time setup fee, and a 60-day free trial on the subscription. The session URL is returned and the customer is redirected to Stripe.
2
Plans and price IDs
Starter (3 locations): $149/mo — price_starter
Growth (10 locations): $299/mo — price_growth
Professional (25 locations): $499/mo — price_pro
Enterprise (50 locations): $799/mo — price_enterprise
Setup fee (all tiers): $500 one-time — price_1TmLfxIjmNR61KaqvjeZaggn
3
After successful payment
Stripe fires a checkout.session.completed webhook to the stripe-webhook Edge Function. This function updates the org's plan and location_limit in the organizations Supabase table. The customer sees their new limit immediately on return.
4
Issuing a refund or credit
Refunds are handled directly in the Stripe dashboard — find the charge and issue a full or partial refund. The setup fee ($500) is non-refundable per Terms of Service. Document all refund decisions in the org's notes in Platform Admin.
📞 Escalation path: Platform issues → admin@medsync.vet → Megan Henley. For Stripe billing disputes, all MedSync team members can access the Stripe dashboard directly.
