For your role

Built for how Scout actually operates.

Different people at Scout have different jobs in MedSync. Here's what the platform does for yours.

Inventory Lead
You own the shelves. MedSync is your system of record.
  • Run weekly cycle counts by A/B/C/D category
  • Log goods lost with full category tracking
  • Monitor expiring lots 60 days out
  • See what's actually on hand, right now
Practice Manager
Your dashboard view across the whole location.
  • Track COGs % against monthly targets
  • Review goods lost totals and trends
  • Check order compliance and cycle count status
  • Pull quarterly and annual spend reports
Veterinarian / Tech
You use it most when something goes wrong with a medication.
  • Log a diagnostic duplicate quickly
  • Check lot expiry before dispensing
  • Flag a goods lost event on the spot
Hospital Receiver
You close the loop between what was ordered and what arrived.
  • Receive shipments against open purchase orders
  • Log lot numbers and expiration dates
  • Flag short shipments or substitutions
  • Reconcile invoices against what was received
Regional / Admin
Cross-location visibility is what MedSync is built for.
  • Compare COGs % across all Scout locations
  • Review the integrity assessment in Reports
  • See who's ordering on time and who isn't
  • Export data for finance and vendor reviews
Why it matters

This is operational infrastructure,
not just a tool.

—
Expired product costs real money
Every lot that expires on a shelf without being logged is an invisible write-off. MedSync tracks every lot from receiving to depletion so nothing disappears quietly.
—
Procurement without data is guesswork
Weekly orders built on muscle memory lead to overstock and stockouts. MedSync ties order history to on-hand counts so ordering decisions are grounded in what's actually there.
—
COGs is the number that matters
Drug spend as a percentage of revenue tells you whether the practice is running lean or leaking. The platform tracks this in real time so you're not surprised at month-end.
—
Reporting creates accountability
When goods lost is logged consistently across Scout, patterns emerge — by location, by category, over time. That data protects the practice and the team.
