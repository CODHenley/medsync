For your role

Built for how
Scout actually works.

Every role at Scout touches inventory differently. MedSync is designed around those differences — what you see, what you log, and what you're accountable for depends on where you fit in the practice.

Leadership
Regional / Admin
You see all four locations in one view. MedSync gives you the cross-location visibility to catch patterns that wouldn't show up at the hospital level — an outlier COGs%, a location that keeps running out of the same product, or a procurement gap that's been flying under the radar.
  • Compare COGs % across all Scout locations side by side
  • Review the portfolio integrity assessment in Reports
  • Monitor order compliance and cycle count status per location
  • Identify goods lost trends by category and location
  • Export data for finance reviews, audits, and vendor negotiations
Hospital Director
Your location's financial health lives in your MedSync dashboard. You don't need to chase your inventory lead for numbers — COGs%, goods lost MTD, cycle count compliance, and order status are surfaced the moment you log in, so you can lead with data, not gut feel.
  • Track COGs % daily against your monthly target
  • Review goods lost totals, trends, and categories
  • Confirm cycle counts are completed on schedule
  • See order status — submitted, received, or overdue
  • Run quarterly and annual spend reports for reviews
Staff
Inventory Lead
You own the shelves — MedSync is your system of record. Everything that comes in, goes out, expires, or disappears gets logged through you. The cycle count schedule, the goods lost log, the lot tracker: these are your tools, and your location's numbers reflect how well you use them.
  • Run weekly cycle counts by A/B/C/D category
  • Log goods lost events with category, quantity, and reason
  • Monitor expiring lots 60 days out and act before they expire
  • Keep live on-hand counts accurate for your location
  • Submit and receive purchase orders against Vetcove
Veterinarian / Tech
You use MedSync at the moment something needs to be documented — when a diagnostic test gets duplicated, when a medication is wasted mid-treatment, or when you pull a lot and want to verify expiry before dispensing. It takes 60 seconds and keeps the record clean.
  • Log a diagnostic duplicate before it becomes a write-off
  • Check lot expiry before dispensing a controlled or cold-chain drug
  • Flag a goods lost event on the spot — waste, spill, or expired
  • Verify on-hand quantity before ordering a restock
Hospital Receiver
You close the loop between what was ordered and what actually arrived. When a Vetcove delivery comes in, you match it against the open PO, log lot numbers and expiry dates, and flag anything that's short or substituted. That data feeds directly into the cycle count and expiry tracking system.
  • Receive shipments line by line against open purchase orders
  • Log lot numbers and expiration dates at point of delivery
  • Flag short shipments, substitutions, or damaged goods
  • Reconcile invoiced items against what was physically received
  • Mark POs complete to update on-hand and lot records
Why it matters

The shelf is a
financial statement.

Expired product is a silent write-off
Every lot that expires without being logged disappears from the books. MedSync tracks every lot from receiving to depletion — nothing goes quietly.
Ordering without data is guesswork
Weekly orders built on habit lead to overstock and stockouts in the same week. MedSync ties order history to real on-hand counts so decisions are grounded.
COGs is the number that matters
Drug spend as a percentage of revenue tells you whether the practice is running lean or leaking. The platform tracks this in real time, not at month-end.
Consistent logging builds accountability
When every location logs goods lost the same way, patterns surface — by location, by product, over time. That data protects the practice and the team.
